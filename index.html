What are Grants?

and How to Get them at FCPS

We are exceptionally good at getting grants.

Since 2010, we have won approximately $26,000,000.00 in competitive grants.

Those grants provided:

  • Reading/Math Assistance
  • Birth-12th grade literacy plan
  • Math & Science Professional Learning
  • 21st Century Community Learning Centers
  • Fresh Fruits and Vegetables for Students
  • World Language Programs/Summer Camps
  • Supports for Boys of Color
  • Field Trips
  • Greenhouses
  • This might be good to ask them to think about things they might want to consider pursuing grants for or to highlight different types of grants.

What Grants ARE About


  • Increasing student achievement
  • Increasing educator knowledge & skills
  • Addressing identified gaps in services
  • Acquiring the tools needed to accomplish your goals
  • Providing equitable access to opportunities to ALL students
  • Grants are about accomplishing goals.
  • Grants provide resources to accomplish those goals.
  • If your need and goal are not compelling, why would an organization give you money for your project?
  • Accomplishing goals requires planning, coordination, and a design for success.
  • We can be your thought partner for your project and help you write it, but ultimately you need to design your project.

What Grants ARE NOT About


  • Replacing positions that have been cut or eliminated
  • Paying on-going salaries for "extra" personnel
  • Spending the funds outside of the parameters of the grant and applicable policies.
  • Examples of unallowed spending:
    • Conditions attached by the grantor
    • Legal obligations
    • Federal, State, District, and School Council policies
  • Grant funds may also restrict the use of materials purchased with grant funds.

How we can help

  • Technical Assistance
  • Grant Writing
  • Proposal Review
  • Grants Database
  • Narrative templates for federal & state grants
  • Preparing budgets for grant applications
  • For larger grants we can write the grant for/with you as well
  • We can also review your proposal and provide feedback from the perspective of a grantreviewer
  • The review must be scheduled in advance and does not substitute for the pre-award grant process

Getting Grants


  • All grants must be approved prior to submission.
  1. Identify a grant that can support you accomplishing your goals
  2. Talk with your supervisor about pursuing the opportunity
  3. Submit an Intent to Apply here: https://bit.ly/2K76DO3
  4. We'll review eligibility requirements and get back to you
  5. We can help you develop the grant, review it, and assist with the submission
  6. Wait for the award decision
